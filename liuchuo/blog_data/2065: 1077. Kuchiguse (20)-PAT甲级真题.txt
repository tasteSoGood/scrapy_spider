<p><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>The Japanese language is notorious for its sentence ending particles. Personal preference of such particles can be considered as a reflection of the speaker’s personality. Such a preference is called “Kuchiguse” and is often exaggerated artistically in Anime and Manga. For example, the artificial sentence ending particle “nyan~” is often used as a stereotype for characters with a cat-like personality:<br></strong></span><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>Itai nyan~ (It hurts, nyan~)</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>Ninjin wa iyada nyan~ (I hate carrots, nyan~)</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>Now given a few lines spoken by the same character, can you find her Kuchiguse?</strong></span></p><p><span style="color: #3366ff;"><strong>Input Specification:<br></strong></span><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>Each input file contains one test case. For each case, the first line is an integer N (2&lt;=N&lt;=100). Following are N file lines of 0~256 (inclusive) characters in length, each representing a character’s spoken line. The spoken lines are case sensitive.</strong></span></p><p><span style="color: #3366ff;"><strong>Output Specification:<br></strong></span><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>For each test case, print in one line the kuchiguse of the character, i.e., the longest common suffix of all N lines. If there is no such suffix, write “nai”.</strong></span></p><p><span style="color: #3366ff;"><strong>Sample Input 1:</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>3</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>Itai nyan~</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>Ninjin wa iyadanyan~</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>uhhh nyan~</strong></span><br><span style="color: #3366ff;"><strong>Sample Output 1:</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>nyan~</strong></span><br><span style="color: #3366ff;"><strong>Sample Input 2:</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>3</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>Itai!</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>Ninjinnwaiyada T_T</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>T_T</strong></span><br><span style="color: #3366ff;"><strong>Sample Output 2:</strong></span><br><span style="color: #33cccc;"><strong>nai</strong></span></p><p><span style="color: #99cc00;"><strong>题目大意：给定N给字符串，求他们的公共后缀，如果不存在公共后缀，就输出“nai”</strong></span><br><span style="color: #ff9900;"><strong>分析：因为是后缀，反过来比较太麻烦，所以每输入一个字符串，就把它逆序过来再比较会比较容易～</strong></span><br><span style="color: #ff9900;"><strong>首先ans = s；后来每输入的一个字符串，都和ans比较，如果后面不相同的就把它截取掉，</strong></span><span style="color: #ff9900;"><strong>最后输出ans即可（要逆序输出～，所以先将ans倒置reverse一下～）</strong></span></p><p></p>
